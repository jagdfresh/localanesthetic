Recognition
Early signs (CNS excitation) Perioral numbness • Tinnitus • Metallic taste • Agitation • Confusion Late signs Seizures • Loss of consciousness • Arrhythmia • Cardiovascular collapse Bupivacaine: CV collapse may precede CNS signs
Management
1. Stop injection immediately
2. Call for help • Airway, O₂, ventilation
3. Seizures: benzodiazepine (avoid propofol if unstable)
4. Lipid emulsion (20% Intralipid) — see calculator above
5. CPR if arrest • Avoid vasopressin, Ca²⁺ blockers, local anesthetics
6. If arrest: prolonged CPR (may take 1+ hour)
7. Consider ECMO if refractory
